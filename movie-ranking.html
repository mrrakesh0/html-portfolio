The Best Movies According to Rakesh

My top 3 movies of all-time.

Mother India (1957)

This epic drama explores themes of poverty, sacrifice, and resilience through the story of a struggling farmer and mother named Radha. Praised for its powerful performances, particularly Nargis as Radha, its breathtaking visuals, and its poignant social commentary, Mother India became the highest-grossing Indian film of all time at the time and remains a cultural touchstone in India..

Sholay (1975)

This masala classic combines action, comedy, romance, and drama into a thrilling tale of two outlaws and their clash with a ruthless bandit leader. Known for its larger-than-life characters, catchy songs, and iconic dialogues, Sholay broke box office records, redefined the commercial Hindi film, and continues to be a beloved favorite for generations of viewers.

3 Idiots

The movie's core story resonates deeply with the audience. It tackles the societal pressure surrounding academic achievement and career choices, particularly in the Indian education system. Through the quirky yet relatable characters, viewers connect with the struggles of finding one's passion and purpose, defying societal expectations, and forging meaningful friendships.
